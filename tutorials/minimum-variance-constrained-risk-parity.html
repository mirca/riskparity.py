Optimization problemÂ¶

riskparity.py allows users to account for the variance of the designed portfolio, i.e., we solve the following optimization problem:

\begin{array}{ll} \underset{\mathbf{w}}{\textsf{minimize}} & R(\mathbf{w}) + \lambda \mathbf{w}^{\top}\boldsymbol{\Sigma}\mathbf{w}\\ \textsf{subject to} & \mathbf{l} \leq \mathbf{w} \leq \mathbf{u}, \texttt{sum}(\mathbf{w}) = 1, \end{array}
[11]:

Sigma = np.cov(log_returns.T)

[12]:

risk_budget = np.ones(len(Sigma)) / len(Sigma)

[13]:

my_portfolio = rp.RiskParityPortfolio(covariance=Sigma, budget=risk_budget)

[48]:

Dmat = np.vstack([np.eye(len(Sigma)), -np.eye(len(Sigma))])
dvec = np.hstack([0.7 * np.ones(len(Sigma)), -0.1 * np.ones(len(Sigma))])
